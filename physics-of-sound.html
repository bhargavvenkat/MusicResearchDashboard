The Fundamentals of Sound

An interactive exploration of the physics behind what we hear.

The Sound Wave

Sound travels as a wave of pressure. We can visualize this as a waveform. The two most fundamental properties of a simple wave are its frequency (how often it repeats) and its amplitude (its intensity).

Use the controls to see and hear how they relate.

Timbre: The Color of Sound

Most sounds are not simple sine waves. The unique shape of a sound's waveform gives it a distinct character, or timbre. This is why a piano and a guitar playing the same note sound different.

Select different waveforms to see how their shape changes the sound's "color".

Interference & Phase

When two sound waves meet, they combine. Their alignment, or phase, determines the result. If their peaks align, they reinforce each other (constructive interference). If a peak aligns with a trough, they can cancel each other out (destructive interference).
